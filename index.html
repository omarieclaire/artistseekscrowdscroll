Does your audience cocreate art with you? yes or no?

I make experimental video games. I was always horrible at games. I played in the local arcade, mashing buttons to victory. I never liked competitive games. If anyone loses I feel the loss, so winning feels terrible.

When I started making games, people didn’t think they were fun. They’d play for a minute and then drift away. They didn’t stay long enough to experience what I was trying to say. So I introduced points, death, and a clock that goes faster and faster. I added all the things I hated and people started liking the games. I still daydream about making something that is raw, unfiltered, unmediated, and untranslated -- but understood. I try. I fail. I try. I fail.

It’s hard to know when to listen to people. When to stop listening. When your art doesn’t reach people, do you translate or transmute your ideas until they are understood?

I make music alone. I sit in my room making strange sounds. Sometimes it sounds so bad my hair stands on end. Sometimes it shimmers with beauty.

When I make music around other people I wonder: Do you like these sounds? What can I do to make it nice for you? I stop exploring and only make sounds I’ve made before, sounds that I think you’ll like.

I go through the motions of art and feel hollow inside. Is it worth it?

yes or no?

I make public art out of a deep desire for connection, a deep desire to be seen, and understood. I find connection terrifying. It’s humbling and horrifying to make a practice where I need other people to bring it to life. My art always feels like fumbling. Like some horrific caricature of the thing I meant to express. Do you listen to your audience? Do you know when they understand? When they feel moved? When they feel left behind?

I enter into conversation with my audience. I fumble through the poetics of connection.
